Phonology

Vowels

Kholosi appears to have a six vowel system judging from extensive recording and formant graphing (which you can see at this link), but there may be some allophony at play. Some of the interesting allophones were:

  • [o̞]: This is the big cluster of dots below the the smaller group of true [o]s in the graph. These appear to be conditioned by a sort of vowel harmony, since they occur in words with repeating /o/s as syllable nuclei.
  • [e]: In syllables with a coda that is a sonorant or no code, /ɛ/ is realised as [e] and usually lengthened.
